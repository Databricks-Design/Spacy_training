### 1. Dataset Health (Volume & Quality)
**The Stat:** 265,977 total transactions.
* **82%** contain usable data (Signal).
* **18%** are empty/junk text (Noise).

> **Stakeholder Significance:**
> This is a **healthy, high-volume dataset**. The 18% "junk" data is actually valuableâ€”it serves as "Negative Sampling," teaching the model to stay quiet when it sees irrelevant text (reducing False Positives).

---

### 2. Complexity & Architecture (The Overlap Issue)
**The Stat:** **0.95%** of transactions (2,053 rows) contain conflicting/overlapping labels.
* **Main Culprit:** `BUSINESS` vs `LOCATION` (e.g., "Dominos NY" where "NY" is both part of the business name and a location).

> **Stakeholder Significance:**
> While 1% seems small, these are often the most critical/complex transactions. Because of this, we **cannot use standard NER**. We must use **SpanCat (Span Categorization)** architecture to ensure we don't drop these details.

---

### 3. Model Configuration (The "Net Size")
**The Stat:** **99.6%** of all entities are between **1 and 7 words** long.
* Most entities (90%) are short (1-3 words).
* The "Long Tail" (descriptions, long business names) caps out at 7 words.

> **Stakeholder Significance:**
> We have precise boundaries for the model. We will configure the model to scan for patterns up to **7 words long**. This maximizes accuracy without wasting computing power on impossibly long phrases.

---

### 4. Risk Assessment (Bias & Imbalance)
**The Stat:** Extreme imbalance between common and rare types.
* **Dominant:** `BUSINESS` (209k examples) & `LOCATION` (115k).
* **At Risk:** `MARKETPLACE` (only **259** examples).

> **Stakeholder Significance:**
> **High Risk of Bias.** The model will learn `BUSINESS` perfectly but will likely ignore `MARKETPLACE` entirely because it is statistically insignificant (0.1% of data).
> **Action Required:** We must artificially boost ("upsample") the Marketplace data, or the model will fail to detect it.
